1919: Aftermath of World War I

A Year of Recovery and Change

World War I Impacts

  • Over 9 million soldiers and 10 million civilians dead
  • Fall of four empires: German, Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, and Russian
  • Massive economic disruption and debt

Post-War Political Landscape

  • Paris Peace Conference began (January)
  • Treaty of Versailles signed (June) - reshaped Europe
  • League of Nations founded to prevent future wars

Social and Economic Consequences

  • Millions of veterans struggling to reintegrate
  • Women's roles changed - suffrage movements gained momentum
  • Global influenza pandemic continued (Spanish Flu)

Technological and Cultural Shifts

  • Advancements in aviation, medicine, and communications
  • Rise of anti-war sentiment and new artistic movements
  • Bauhaus school founded, influencing modern design
